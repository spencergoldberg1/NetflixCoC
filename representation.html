Representation

The representation in Netflix's original productions highlights the platform's commitment to diversity, though it reveals areas where improvement is necessary. A comprehensive study by the Annenberg Inclusion Initiative, conducted in collaboration with Netflix, analyzed the platform's U.S. original live-action films (126) and series (180) released in 2018 and 2019. This study provides a detailed overview of on-screen inclusion across various metrics such as gender, race/ethnicity, LGBTQ+ identities, and disability.

In terms of gender representation, Netflix has achieved notable parity in leading roles. The study found that 52% of all leads and co-leads across film and series were women (Annenberg Inclusion Initiative, USC Annenberg). This is a significant achievement, especially considering the broader industry statistics, where only 7.6% of top-grossing film directors were women during the same period (Annenberg Inclusion Initiative, USC Annenberg).

However, the representation of racial and ethnic groups in Netflix's content shows mixed results. While the platform has improved the representation of Black characters, with 15.2% of stories overall featuring Black leads/co-leads, the representation of Latinx characters falls short. Latinx characters represented only 2.6% of leads/co-leads and 4.5% of the main cast, underrepresenting their proportion in the U.S. population, which is significantly higher (Annenberg Inclusion Initiative, USC Annenberg).

The study also reveals a gap in the representation of the LGBTQ+ community and people with disabilities. These groups are significantly underrepresented in Netflix content compared to their proportions in the general population, indicating a need for more inclusive storytelling. For instance, only 2.3% of all leads/co-leads and 5.3% of main cast characters were identified as LGBTQ+, while people with disabilities featured in only 5.3% of stories (Annenberg Inclusion Initiative, USC Annenberg).

Overall, while Netflix demonstrates a commitment to gender and racial diversity, these findings underscore the need for more inclusive representation, particularly for LGBTQ+ and disabled communities. The challenges faced by Netflix in this area reflect a broader industry trend and highlight the importance of continuous efforts towards more inclusive representation in media.
