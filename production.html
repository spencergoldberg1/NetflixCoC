Production

Netflix's progression in content production epitomizes a strategic transformation that has significantly reshaped the streaming and entertainment industry. This evolution began with a shift from licensing content to investing in original productions. The launch of "House of Cards" in 2013 marked the onset of Netflix's journey into original programming, setting a new course for the company's content strategy ("Netflixâ€™s Business Strategy Explained" Starting Business).

Over the years, Netflix's content strategy has been characterized by substantial investment and a consistent increase in original productions. In 2022, Netflix released 935 original first-run titles, representing a 68% increase from the previous year. This rise in content is indicative of Netflix's commitment to providing a diverse and expansive library. From 2017 to 2022, Netflix's original production hours increased dramatically, with a significant shift in focus towards non-U.S. productions, reflecting the company's global expansion ambitions (TBI Tech & Analysis: The where & why of Netflix's original content spend - TBI Vision).

Netflix's original content strategy also involves a significant emphasis on international productions. As of March 2022, over 50% of the titles in Netflix's U.S. catalogue were Originals or Exclusives. This strategic move has been driven by the rise of studio-led direct-to-consumer platforms and the shrinking pool of licensable content. Investing in original content allows Netflix to offer exclusive titles internationally without additional licensing costs, which is crucial as the company seeks to grow its international subscriber base (Ampere Analysis Insights).

The company's flexibility in content production and promotion has been a key advantage over traditional linear networks. Netflix's approach allows for nurturing shows over time and providing a platform for more creative storytelling, such as varying episode runtimes and flexible season structures. This flexibility, combined with personalized content promotion, has enabled Netflix to sustain interest in its series long after their premiere, contributing to the platform's popularity and success (Netflix - Overview - Long-Term View).

In conclusion, Netflix's production strategy, marked by a significant investment in original content, technological innovation, and a global focus, has not only disrupted traditional models of content creation but also set new standards for streaming platforms. Through its strategic content production, Netflix has redefined the entertainment landscape, offering an unparalleled viewing experience and continually adapting to the evolving preferences of its global audience.
