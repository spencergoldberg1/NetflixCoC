Regulation

Netflix employs geoblocking to manage its diverse content libraries, which vary across different countries. This practice, driven by licensing agreements and rights, ensures that certain content is available only in specific regions. As of 2023, Netflix is available for streaming in over 190 countries, but its library varies based on the country and changes over time (Netflix Help Center).

To enforce geoblocking, Netflix has implemented measures to prevent users from accessing geo-restricted content via VPNs and proxies. This is part of the company's strategy to comply with the terms of its licensing deals with content creators and networks. Despite this, VPNs are commonly used to bypass Netflix's geoblocking, leading to an ongoing technological battle between Netflix and VPN service providers (Robots.net, Privacy Savvy).

The library size of Netflix also varies by region. As an example, the Netflix library in the US had 6,074 titles in 2021. This number is subject to change due to the rights they hold and can significantly differ in other regions (Comparitech).

In conclusion, Netflix's use of geoblocking and the variation in content availability reflect the complexities of digital content distribution in the streaming era. These practices highlight the challenges Netflix faces in balancing licensing agreements, regional content preferences, and the technological measures needed to enforce these regulations.
